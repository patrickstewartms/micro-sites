Message from MS Society

Scroll down

Dear friends,

Welcome to the 19th Annual RBC Golf Tournament. When you choose to participate in this event, you choose to engage in the fight to end MS. We can’t thank you enough for being present today and raising funds for world-leading MS research.

Nineteen years have passed since RBC first partnered with us to raise money for Canadian MS research, and much has happened in those 19 years. People living with relapsing-remitting MS have more treatment options than they ever have before, and this is directly because of people like you who have invested in the research required to improve quality of life for people living with the disease. We have since prioritized investments in progressive MS research, as people living with the most severe form of MS currently do not have treatment options to slow or stop progression of disability. This is a reality we are working hard to change, and we are optimistic that newly established international research collaborations such as the Progressive MS Alliance will accelerate the development of treatments that are urgently needed for many Canadians living with progressive MS.

Last year we announced a fresh round of funding for the Canadian Pediatric Demyelinating Disease Network ($3.2 million) for Dr. Brenda Banwell’s extensive study of pediatric MS. The study will lead to a better understanding of many aspects of MS, including early triggers of the disease and ways to improve quality of life for children and teens who live with MS. To build on the excitement of this comprehensive study, we have since announced funding for Dr. Helen Tremlett’s gut microbiome study, which will leverage access to the same cohort of adolescents and teens to discover more about the interactions between gut bacteria and MS disease activity. This unique collaboration will help to drive large-scale advances in research that will ultimately benefit both children and adults living with MS.

Canada may have the highest rate of MS in the world, but we are also home to some of the world’s best MS research. This is what gives us hope. This is why we believe a cure for MS is achievable, and it’s why we will continue to do whatever it takes to find a cure for this disease.

Thank you for joining us in the fight to end MS.




Lisa McCoy
President, Ontario & Nunavut Division
MS Society of Canada


Yves Savoie
President and Chief Executive Officer
MS Society of Canada
